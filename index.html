Course Project

Promotion Sensitivity Analysis Based on Subscription Status

Empirical Analytics · Korea University

Problem Identified ambiguity in promotional behavior by recognizing that both subscribers and non‑subscribers could theoretically show stronger or weaker conversion rate increases or faster/slower decision times during promotions, highlighting the lack of empirical evidence on their actual behavioral differences.
Solution Conducted empirical analysis using JD.com’s March 2018 transaction‑level dataset, applying DID methodology with Parallel Trend and Placebo Tests to compare changes in conversion rate and decision time by subscription status.
Impact Demonstrated that subscribers respond more strategically and efficiently during promotions, providing evidence that differentiated promotional strategies for subscribers vs. non‑subscribers are empirically justified.
